## ⚠️ THINGS YOU CHECK — BUT NOT SAFELY

These will bite you in evaluation if left as-is.

### 7. **Spaces inside the map (VERY IMPORTANT)**

Right now:

* You allow spaces
* But you **do NOT validate that spaces are enclosed**

❌ This is a classic Cub3D fail case:

```
111111
10   01
111111
```

Spaces are basically “void”.
**Any `0` or player touching a space = map is open**

➡️ You MUST check:

* If a tile is `0` or `NSEW`
* All 4 neighbors exist
* And are NOT spaces

🧠 **This requires a full 2D map**, not line-by-line validation.

---

### 8. **Different row lengths**

Your map can be:

```
1111111
10001
1111111
```

This passes your current checks ❌
But it’s **invalid** — implicit holes.

➡️ You must:

* Store all map lines
* Pad them to max width with spaces
* Validate neighbors safely

---

### 9. **You don’t validate the *inside* of the map**

Right now you only validate:

* borders
* characters

❌ You do NOT check:

* holes inside the map
* diagonal leaks
* open paths to void

➡️ Subject expects a **closed map everywhere**

👉 **Line-by-line validation is NOT enough for Cub3D.**
You *must* do a **post-parse full map validation pass**.

Best approach (industry-standard for 42):

2. Pad rows

   * If neighbor is space or out of bounds → ❌
